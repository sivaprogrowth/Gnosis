# AI Search Decision Journey

Empirical map of how consumers use AI-powered search across the full purchase decision journey — not just at the top of the funnel. Based on McKinsey's AI Discovery Survey (August 2025, n=1,927 US consumers) from [[new-front-door-to-the-internet]].

## The seven-stage usage map

| Journey stage | Use case | % of respondents | Example query |
|---|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Learning broadly about category / brands / products | **73%** | "What should I consider when choosing a credit card?" |
| Awareness | Planning for trip or special occasion | 53% | "What's a good 7-day itinerary?" |
| Consideration | Explaining features or technical specifications | 60% | "What does '4K resolution' mean on a TV?" |
| Consideration | Generating personalized recommendations | 57% | "What's the best laptop under $800 for a college student?" |
| Decision | Comparing specific products | **61%** | "Compare iPhone 15 vs Galaxy S24 side by side" |
| Decision | Summarizing reviews for specific products | 60% | "What do most people like or dislike about this mattress?" |

## Strategic implication

Traditional AI search coverage tends to focus on **awareness-stage content** ("how AI answers beat SEO for learning about a category"). The data shows this understates the problem: **60–61% of users rely on AI for decision-stage tasks** — comparison and review summarization. These are the last-mile, highest-intent moments that historically occurred on the brand's own PDP or on review sites.

For GEO strategy, this means:

- **Comparison-triggering content must exist on earned sources** — a brand can no longer assume that consumers land on its own comparison page. AI has pre-emptively synthesized one.
- **Review summaries must be accurate at the source level** — if an AI summarizes negative sentiment, tracing back to which third-party reviews anchored the summary is critical.
- **Feature-explanation content is high-leverage** — clear, neutral specification explanations get lifted directly into AI answers (60% use case).

## Sector-level AI adoption

Per McKinsey, share of respondents using AI "mainly or exclusively" for purchases:

| Sector | Mainly+Exclusively AI |
|---|---|
| Consumer electronics & appliances | 24% |
| Travel and hospitality | 23% |
| Grocery (food & beverage) | 21% |
| Wellness, nutrition, health tech | 21% |
| Financial services and products | 19% |
| Beauty, skincare, personal care | 19% |
| Apparel, footwear, accessories | 16% |

Consumer electronics leads — consistent with Chen et al.'s finding in [[geo-how-to-dominate-ai-search]] that AI search produces the sharpest Brand/Earned/Social divergence vs Google in this vertical.

## Generational shift

- Gen Z: slightly higher preference for social media (14%).
- Baby boomers: **only generation still preferring traditional search** (45%) over AI-powered search (36%).
- Everyone else: AI search is the top digital source for buying decisions.
