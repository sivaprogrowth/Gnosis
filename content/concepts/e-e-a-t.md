Originally a Google Search Quality Rater concept used to evaluate content quality. Now — per [[geo-how-to-dominate-ai-search]] — **no longer an abstract SEO framework** but a set of **direct, concrete inputs to AI search engines' recommendation algorithms**.

## The four dimensions

- **Experience** — the content creator has first-hand experience with the subject (used the product, provided the service, lived the claim).
- **Expertise** — the creator demonstrates subject-matter competence (credentials, depth, technical accuracy).
- **Authoritativeness** — the creator (or domain) is cited and recognized by other authorities in the field.
- **Trustworthiness** — the content is accurate, transparent, and can be relied upon; the site has clear ownership, editorial policies, and contact info.

## Why E-E-A-T is newly operational for GEO

Per §3.2.3:

> *"'Emerging consumer trust' in high-value decisions is the ultimate testament to the influence of AI recommendations. However, this trust is fragile and contingent on the AI's perceived reliability, which is built on the credibility of its sources. The implication is that brand authority and E-E-A-T are no longer abstract SEO concepts. They are direct inputs into the AI's decision-making algorithm."*

A brand the AI perceives as less authoritative or trustworthy **will be excluded from high-consideration recommendations**.

## Concrete actions that build AI-perceived E-E-A-T

- **Earn backlinks from reputable sources** — not just for PageRank, but as direct trust signals to AI. Feeds [[earned-media-bias]].
- **Secure positive reviews on third-party platforms** — Trustpilot, Google Reviews, vertical-specific review sites.
- **Get products/services featured in expert roundups** — "best X for Y" editorial pieces that AI ranks against.
- **Create deep, expert-level content that demonstrates knowledge** — not marketing copy, but substantive analysis that other authorities cite.
- **Publish with named authors with verifiable credentials and online presence** — an article by "Dr. Jane Smith, 20 years in the field, with her linked LinkedIn and published papers" carries more weight than an unattributed blog post.
- **Maintain editorial transparency** — clearly stated review methodology, conflict-of-interest disclosures, fact-check processes.

## The shift from SEO E-E-A-T to GEO E-E-A-T

In traditional SEO, E-E-A-T influences rankings indirectly, via quality raters and many other signals. In AI search, E-E-A-T becomes **the primary filter** determining whether your site is cited at all:

- AI engines cite fewer sources per answer (3–10 vs Google's 10 blue links).
- AI engines **synthesize a single response**, so source trust is compressed into a harder cutoff.
- AI engines explicitly optimize for accuracy and avoid "low-trust" sources to prevent hallucination.

## Engine-level sensitivity

- [[chatgpt]] and [[claude]] — most E-E-A-T-sensitive; heavy reliance on editorial authority.
- [[perplexity]] — still sensitive, but accepts broader signals (YouTube creators with authority, community reputation).
- [[gemini]] — inherits Google's E-E-A-T signals via grounding; most continuous with traditional SEO E-E-A-T mental model.
