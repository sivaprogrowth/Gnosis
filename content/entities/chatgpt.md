OpenAI's conversational AI and AI search engine. Holds ~81% global market share of AI chatbot traffic as of July 2025 (StatCounter) and receives ~3.1B visits/month (SimilarWeb, Sept 2024). The variant tested in the GEO paper is `gpt-4o-search-preview`.

## Behavior as an AI search engine

Per [[geo-how-to-dominate-ai-search]]:

- **Most extreme [[earned-media-bias]] of any engine tested.** ~93.5% Earned for well-known brands, ~95.1% for niche brands. Brand content only 5–7%, Social content effectively **0%**.
- **Near-zero cross-language domain overlap** — effectively switches its entire site ecosystem by language. GPT "localizes" most aggressively of all engines; English authority does not transfer.
- **Concentrated top-domain distribution** — citations concentrate on a narrow set of authoritative editorial sites (Wikipedia, TechRadar, Tom's Guide, Consumer Reports, Car and Driver). Wikipedia ~19.7% of citations in one study.
- **Moderate paraphrase stability** — results stable across rewordings; domain overlap 0.3–0.7 typical. Language changes affect results much more than paraphrasing.
- **Informational queries: Earned-heavy, near-zero Social.** Consideration: almost entirely Earned. Transactional: Brand rises but still Earned secondary.
- **Automotive specifics:** top domains include Wikipedia, Automoblog, news outlets (AP) — mixed with some retail-oriented sources. Mean article age ~similar to Claude (high hundreds of days).

## GEO implications for this engine

1. Invest in **earned media relationships** with top-tier English publishers *and* local-language equivalents per target market.
2. Wikipedia presence matters — ensure accuracy and references.
3. Brand-owned content helps little unless heavily cited elsewhere.
4. For [[big-brand-bias]]: niche players face the steepest hill here — GPT defaults to market leaders unless strong editorial validation exists.
5. Track citations per vertical via [[citation-network-mapping]] — GPT's short-list is narrow and measurable.
