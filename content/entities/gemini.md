# Gemini

Google's conversational AI, with Google Search grounding. ~2% global AI chatbot share (July 2025 StatCounter). The variant tested in the GEO paper is `gemini-2.5-flash` with the `google_search` tool enabled via generation config.

## Behavior as an AI search engine

Per [[geo-how-to-dominate-ai-search]]:

- **Most brand-forward of the AI engines.** For well-known brands: ~63.4% Earned, ~25.1% Brand, ~11.5% Social. For niche: ~66.4% Earned, ~21.2% Brand, ~12.7% Social. Meaningfully more willing to cite brand-owned properties than [[chatgpt]] or [[claude]].
- **Widest domain diversity** — in many verticals, Gemini surfaces the largest number of distinct domains per query set (e.g., 98 in Auto Repair, 151 in Dentists, 108 in Moving Companies).
- **Balanced paraphrase/language behavior** — cross-language stability is moderate (higher than GPT, lower than Claude).
- **Bank queries: most brand-leaning** of AI engines — Bank of America, U.S. Bank surface as brand outliers within the top 10. Brand shares frequently at or above half of citations for family persona.
- **Still earned-dominant on average** — editorial hubs (Bankrate, NerdWallet, CNBC, Forbes, Yahoo Finance, Business Insider) anchor most ranking answers.

## GEO implications for this engine

1. **Brand-owned content pays off on Gemini more than on other AI engines.** Deep, well-structured site content (detailed specs, FAQ, schema markup) gets cited.
2. Gemini rewards a dual strategy: top earned-media placements + strong owned content on your own domain.
3. Diversity of citations means competitive surface is larger — more opportunities, but also more competition to manage.
4. Inherits Google's signal set to some degree via grounding — traditional Google SEO foundations still pay dividends on Gemini.
