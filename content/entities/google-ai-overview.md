# Google AI Overview (AIO)

The AI-generated summary surface Google injects at the top of search results for qualifying queries. Formerly called Search Generative Experience (SGE). Distinct from [[google-search]]'s traditional blue-link results and distinct from [[gemini]] as a standalone chatbot — AIO is a hybrid surface that sits *inside* Google Search.

## Scale and impact

- **~50% of Google searches already have AI summaries**, expected to rise to >75% by 2028 ([[new-front-door-to-the-internet]], McKinsey).
- **~15% of observed queries surface an AI summary** in real-world logs; link CTR falls to ~8% when a summary is present vs ~15% without ([[geo-how-to-dominate-ai-search]], citing Pew Research data).
- **~1% of clicks occur inside the AI box.**
- **~26% of AI-summary sessions end the session without any click** — the "zero-click" outcome.

## Behavior as an AI search surface

Per [[new-front-door-to-the-internet]]'s analysis of AIO source mix (July 2025, ~600 consumer purchase questions per industry):

| Vertical | Affiliate blogs | UGC | News/Media | Brand/Retailer | Academic |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Consumer packaged goods | 50% | 15% | 10% | 10% | 10% |
| E-commerce | **80%** | 5% | <5% | 10% | 5% |
| Financial services | 50% | 10% | 15% | 20% | <5% |
| Telecommunications | 15% | **45%** | 20% | 10% (split retailer+brand) | 10% |

**Affiliate-blog dominance is the defining AIO pattern** in e-commerce and CPG. Telecommunications is the outlier — UGC-heavy (Reddit, forums, YouTube). Financial services uniquely credits brand/retailer content (20%) more than other verticals.

## Contrast with other AI surfaces

- vs [[chatgpt]] / [[claude]]: AIO includes more affiliate blogs and UGC; ChatGPT/Claude skew heavily to top-tier editorial (TechRadar, Tom's Guide, Consumer Reports).
- vs [[perplexity]]: Perplexity is more YouTube-weighted; AIO is more affiliate-blog-weighted.
- vs [[gemini]] (standalone): Gemini is a full chatbot; AIO is a search-result-integrated summary. Different corpus, different real-estate.
- vs [[google-search]] blue links: AIO displaces some traffic from the first-page organic results.

## Naming nuance: "Google AI Mode" vs "Google AI Overview"

Google has shipped multiple AI-flavored surfaces within Search with overlapping names:

- **Google AI Overview (AIO)** — the summary box inserted above organic results for qualifying queries. The subject of this page.
- **Google AI Mode** — a distinct conversational mode within Google Search covered by [[semrush-ai-visibility-index]]. More chatbot-like; appears as a dedicated surface rather than inline.
- **SGE (Search Generative Experience)** — the earlier name for the AIO experiment phase.

These are related products but measured distinctly. When citing a source, verify which surface it evaluated. For ProGrowth client work, track both AIO and AI Mode — they have different citation ecologies.

## GEO implications for this engine

1. **Affiliate-publisher placements are the #1 leverage point** for AIO visibility in CPG and e-commerce — not brand-owned content, not review sites. This is a specific tactical target (e.g., Wirecutter, NerdWallet, category-specific affiliate sites).
2. **Telecom/community-driven verticals**: Reddit and YouTube presence matters.
3. **Traditional Google SEO still provides the foundation** — AIO draws from the same crawl base. Being in the top organic results correlates with being cited in AIO for many queries.
4. **Monitor AIO appearance rate per query** — not every query triggers AIO, and triggering rates are shifting rapidly (Google has cut back AIO coverage in some verticals).
