Traditional web search engine. Still holds ~90% global search market share as of mid-2025. Used as the baseline in the GEO paper via its Programmable (Custom) Search API, top-10 web results per query.

## Behavior vs AI search engines

Per [[geo-how-to-dominate-ai-search]]:

- **Balanced Brand / Earned / Social mix** across intents — contrasts sharply with AI engines' earned-heavy bias.
  - Informational: ~30% Brand, ~38% Earned, ~32% Social.
  - Consideration: ~10% Brand, ~50% Earned, ~40% Social.
  - Transactional: ~40% Brand, ~35% Earned, ~25% Social.
- **Significant Social share** — Reddit, Quora, YouTube appear with meaningful weight, especially in consumer electronics and family SUV verticals.
- **Higher brand share for transactional queries** — "buy iPhone 15 online" surfaces the brand site; AI engines still route through editorial reviews.
- **Most stable across paraphrases** in imperative/keyword formats (overlaps peak ~0.73). But across language changes, Google is *less* stable than Claude or Perplexity — it tilts strongly toward English.
- **Locale-sensitive Social presence** — significant Social in English, smaller in non-English results.

## GEO implications (contrast lens)

1. Traditional SEO — technical foundations, keyword relevance, ranking links — is still how you win Google.
2. Google's Social inclusivity means Reddit/Quora/community presence is a real traffic driver where AI engines (esp. [[chatgpt]], [[claude]]) provide zero Social exposure.
3. Clients who've been doing Google SEO well are under-prepared for AI search's earned-media-first reality. The playbooks diverge.
4. AI Overviews in Google results (~15% of queries) are eroding click share from Google itself — ~26% of AI-summary sessions end with zero clicks.
