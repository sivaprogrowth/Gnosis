AI-native search engine. ~8% global AI chatbot share (July 2025 StatCounter). Disclosed 780M monthly queries in May 2025. The variant tested in the GEO paper is `sonar-pro` with `search_mode=web`, `search_context_size=medium`.

## Behavior as an AI search engine

Per [[geo-how-to-dominate-ai-search]]:

- **Most balanced of the AI engines — but still earned-dominant.** For well-known brands: ~67.4% Earned, ~23.8% Social, ~8.8% Brand. For niche: ~74.3% Earned, ~17.5% Social, ~9.1% Brand. The only AI engine with meaningful Social share.
- **YouTube is a major citation source** — in automotive queries, YouTube was Perplexity's single most frequent domain (~8.9%). Also cites Reddit, Quora, and retail/review sites heavily.
- **More commercially linked and fresher** — includes retail and aftermarket sites (autozone.com, cars.com) alongside editorial outlets. Article freshness higher than [[chatgpt]]/[[claude]] but with some loss of coverage stability.
- **Stronger Brand presence in informational queries** (~50.6% Brand in one split) than other AI engines.
- **Moderate cross-language stability** — higher than [[chatgpt]] (which swaps ecosystems entirely), lower than [[claude]] (which reuses English).
- **Diverse long-tail citation profile** — distributes citations across many domains rather than concentrating on a handful. ~60.6% of citations fall in the "Others" long tail.

## GEO implications for this engine

1. **YouTube strategy is a real lever for Perplexity visibility** — high-quality expert video content, product reviews, comparisons. Unique to Perplexity among AI engines.
2. Community presence matters more here — Reddit threads, Quora answers, high-quality forum contributions can surface.
3. Retail/aftermarket domain presence (clean product pages, review integrations) helps for transactional queries.
4. Still an earned-media game, but the *form* of earned media is broader than [[claude]]/[[chatgpt]].
5. Perplexity may be the **easiest engine for niche brands** to break into because of the broader citation ecology.
