## Abstract

Empirical comparative study of AI search engines ([[chatgpt]], [[claude]], [[perplexity]], [[gemini]]) versus [[google-search]], conducted August 2025 by University of Toronto researchers. The central empirical finding: AI engines exhibit a systematic and overwhelming bias toward Earned media (third-party authoritative sources) over Brand-owned and Social content — a stark contrast to Google's more balanced mix. From this, the authors derive a strategic [[generative-engine-optimization]] agenda built on four imperatives and a five-pillar operating system.

## Key claims

- **[[earned-media-bias]] is the dominant empirical pattern.** Across verticals, regions, and languages, AI engines favor third-party editorial/review sources. ChatGPT most extreme (~93–95% Earned), Claude similar (~87%), Perplexity most balanced (but still earned-dominant with meaningful Social share 17–24%), Gemini most brand-forward of the AI set (63–66% Earned). Google balances Brand/Earned/Social.
- **Engine identity > prompt wording.** Different AI engines draw from different site ecosystems. Claude/Perplexity share some overlap; GPT/Perplexity barely intersect. Language shifts results much more than paraphrasing does.
- **Four strategic GEO imperatives:** (1) engineer for agency and scannability ([[api-able-brand]]); (2) dominate earned media to build AI-perceived [[e-e-a-t]]; (3) adopt engine-specific tactics; (4) pursue a localized authority strategy for multilingual markets.
- **Content must be "justifiable" by the AI.** AI synthesizes a shortlist, not 10 blue links. Whoever provides clear [[justification-attributes]] — comparison tables, bolded value props, pros/cons — wins the recommendation.
- **[[big-brand-bias]] disadvantages niche players.** Unbranded queries default to market leaders (cola: 62% major brands combined, ChatGPT 56%, Perplexity 68%). Niche brands must over-invest in deep niche authority and grassroots signals.
- **GEO is an ongoing managed service.** Authors propose a 5-pillar operating system: continuous [[citation-network-mapping]], structured justification framework, authority-building pipeline, offensive/defensive ranking defense, integrated metrics dashboard.
- **"From retrieval to agency."** Users increasingly delegate actions (buy, book, negotiate) to AI, requiring clean structured data across the full customer lifecycle.

## Methodology

- **Engines tested:** Perplexity (sonar-pro with web search), Claude (3.5 Sonnet with web_search tool), Gemini (2.5-flash with Google Search grounding), GPT (4o-search-preview). Google via Custom Search API as baseline.
- **Query corpus:** ~1,000 consumer ranking prompts ("Top 10 X", "Best Y in Z") across 10 categories, two regions (US, Canada), across verticals (consumer electronics, automotive, software products, local services, banking).
- **Classification:** Each cited domain labeled Brand / Earned / Social via [[brand-earned-social-taxonomy]] (GPT-4o-assisted).
- **Metrics:** Top-k domain overlap (Coverage@k), Jaccard domain overlap, aggregate domain-type distributions, freshness (article age), cross-language / cross-paraphrase stability.
