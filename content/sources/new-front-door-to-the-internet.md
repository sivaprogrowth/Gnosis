# New Front Door to the Internet: Winning in the Age of AI Search

## Abstract

Business-oriented report from McKinsey's Growth Marketing & Sales Practice quantifying the economic stakes of AI-powered search and prescribing a four-move framework for brand activation. Pairs consumer survey data (AI Discovery Survey, August 2025, n=1,927 US consumers) with a CMO survey (September 2025, n≈30 Fortune 500 consumer brand CMOs). Complements the more rigorous academic treatment in [[geo-how-to-dominate-ai-search]] with executive-oriented framing, projections, and vertical-level source-mix data for [[google-ai-overview]].

## Key claims

- **Economic stakes**: $750B of US revenue will flow through AI-powered search by 2028 (McKinsey projection). ~50% of Google searches already have AI summaries today, rising to >75% by 2028. Unprepared brands face 20–50% traffic decline from traditional search.
- **AI search is already the #1 preferred source of insight**: 44% of AI users rank it primary, topping traditional search (31%), retailer/brand sites (9%), review sites (6%), social (5%), live commerce (2%), editorial content (2%). Only baby boomers still prefer traditional search (45% vs 36% AI).
- **Usage spans the full [[ai-search-decision-journey]]**: 73% for awareness, 60–61% for comparison/specs, 60% for review summarization. 70%+ of AI search users ask top-of-funnel questions.
- **Consumer electronics sector leads adoption** (24% mainly/exclusively AI for purchases). Grocery, travel, wellness all 21–23%. Apparel and beauty lag at 16–19%.
- **Brand-owned sites comprise only 5–10% of AI-cited sources** — strong reinforcement of [[earned-media-bias]]. Source mix varies dramatically by vertical:
  - Consumer packaged goods: 50% affiliate blogs, 15% UGC, 10% news/media, 10% brand/retailer, 10% academic.
  - E-commerce: **80% affiliate blogs** (dominant), 10% brand, 5% UGC, 5% academic.
  - Financial services: 50% affiliate, 20% brand, 15% news, 10% UGC.
  - Telecommunications: 45% UGC, 20% news/media, 15% brand, 10% each academic and retailer.
- **Traditional brand strength ≠ AI visibility**: see [[brand-strength-ai-visibility-gap]]. Sportswear audit (5 anonymized brands) showed 3 losing AI share-of-voice vs market share (−0.3 to −0.6) and 2 winning (+0.4, +0.6). Market share does not predict AI surface area.
- **CMO readiness gap**: only 16% of brands systematically track AI search performance. GEO performance of even industry leaders may lag SEO by 20–50%.
- **McKinsey's 4-move GEO framework**:
  1. Undertake a robust diagnostic (maps to [[citation-network-mapping]]).
  2. Adjust content investments across owned / third-party / communities.
  3. Optimize content for AI surface (credibility, clear headings, precise language, LLM-optimized).
  4. Invest in GEO as a core capability — cross-functional team, GEO-specific KPIs, updated tech infrastructure.

## Methodological note

McKinsey uses a **different, more granular source taxonomy** than Chen et al.: affiliate blogs, user-generated content, news/media, brand/retailer, academic/market research, other. "Affiliate blogs" — the dominant category for CPG and E-commerce — has no clean equivalent in [[brand-earned-social-taxonomy]]'s three-bucket scheme. See that page for reconciliation.

## Forward-looking observations

- LLMs will begin testing paid ad formats within AI search.
- AI agents will become *consumers* of GEO output — autonomously making purchase decisions on behalf of human users. Reinforces [[api-able-brand]] as critical infrastructure.
