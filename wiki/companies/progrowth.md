# ProGrowth

An AI-first fractional marketing operations company. Operates as an embedded marketing function for B2B companies rather than advising from the outside.

## Services

- **Fractional CMO** — embedded marketing leadership, not offshore advisory.
- **AI marketing** — LLM-assisted content production, AI video generation, AI-native SEO (GEO).
- **Marketing automation** — CRM (Zoho Bigin), booking systems, SMS/email reminder pipelines, ops tooling.
- **AI video generation & editing** — short-form creative for LinkedIn, paid media, sales enablement.
- **Analytics & attribution** — Matomo (self-hosted), Google Analytics, custom dashboards.

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Mid-market B2B in verticals that are historically underserved by modern marketing tooling:

- B2B tech / SaaS (early- to mid-growth stage)
- Financial services (commercial lending, wealth, insurance brokerages)
- Credit unions and community banks
- Insurance carriers and agencies
- Professional services (legal, accounting, consulting)

## Strategic bet

Three structural shifts converge in ProGrowth's favor:

1. **AI-search disruption.** Traditional SEO assumptions break (see [[earned-media-bias]], [[brand-strength-ai-visibility-gap]]). Clients need operators who can execute a [[generative-engine-optimization]] playbook, not just talk about it.
2. **Fractional is structurally better than full-time for mid-market.** The $300K/year CMO doesn't fit a $3–15M revenue business. A fractional operator running an AI-augmented stack does.
3. **Toolchain is productizable.** The same stack ProGrowth runs for clients ([[ai-overview-tool]], Matomo, Bigin automations, Gnosis) becomes an asset.

## Competitive positioning

- vs [[marketri]] — pure advisory, no embedded execution, weak on AI ([[marketri]] page has deeper breakdown).
- vs [[semrush]] — Semrush ships tools; ProGrowth ships *outcomes*. Complementary rather than competitive for now.
- vs generalist fractional firms — ProGrowth is AI-native by default.

## ProGrowth as an [[ai-native-services|AI-Native Services (AINS)]] company

Per the [[ai-native-services-playbook|Emergence Capital Spring 2026 playbook]], ProGrowth is structurally an AINS business — it absorbs the full marketing-operations stack and sells the outcome (pipeline, MQLs, SQLs, content throughput), not the software. This framing gives ProGrowth a vocabulary and diagnostic framework distinct from traditional fractional-marketing firms.

### Diagnostic tests to run quarterly

- **[[mirage-pmf]] check** — is revenue growth powered by AI leverage or by added human headcount? Honest COGS accounting is the pre-requisite: inference spend + model APIs + human-in-the-loop delivery time all belong in COGS, not opex.
- **Revenue per Employee (ARR/FTE)** — should be meaningfully higher than a traditional fractional firm's. If it isn't, AI isn't pulling weight.
- **Gross margin trend** — should expand with scale, not stay flat.

### Candidate [[north-star-product-metric|north-star product metrics]]

- **Campaigns shipped per delivery-FTE per month** — direct throughput leverage
- **Content pieces per human-author-hour** — AI-assisted production leverage
- **Client-health-score improvement per delivery-hour** — outcome-leverage rather than throughput-leverage

One must be chosen and tracked to make the Mirage PMF test actionable.

### Pricing transition ([[outcome-based-pricing]])

ProGrowth currently uses labor-based retainers. AINS playbook guidance: set an explicit timeline to transition to outcome-based pricing as the AI matures. Two models fit:

- **Credits-based for continuous work** — campaigns, content pieces, qualified leads as credit units
- **Project-based fixed-fee for discrete work** — website launches, rebrands, one-time migrations

[[ai-overview-tool]] is the **Palantir-pattern leave-behind** — recurring-revenue product asset between services engagements. Already aligned with the playbook.

### ICP validation

ProGrowth's downmarket focus (mid-market B2B — credit unions, insurance, professional services; *not* Fortune 500) aligns with the playbook's **"downmarket often productizes faster"** thesis. Lower ACVs force productization; homogeneous workflows reduce edge cases; simpler engagements demonstrate AI leverage faster.
